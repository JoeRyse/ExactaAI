2.2 POST POSITION IMPACT BY TRACK SIZE
HALF-MILE TRACKS (Yonkers, Northfield, Freehold, Saratoga)
CRITICAL: Post position is nearly DETERMINISTIC
Posts 1-2: STRONG ADVANTAGE (+5 to +7 points).
Posts 7-8: SEVERE DISADVANTAGE (-8 to -12 points).
Reality: Rail horse makes lead 80%+ of the time.
7/8 & 1-MILE TRACKS (Mohawk, Meadowlands)
The "Long Stretch" Factor:
The stretch is 1,000+ feet. There is ample time to close.
Posts 1-3: Slight Advantage (+2).
Posts 4-7: Neutral.
Posts 8-10: PLAYABLE in Trots and higher-class Paces if horse has closing speed.
Action: Do NOT deduct points for Posts 8-10 at Mohawk/Meadowlands if the horse has a Last Quarter (L1/4) under :27.4.
SECTION 3: GAIT-SPECIFIC PROTOCOLS
3.1 PACER VS. TROTTER FUNDAMENTALS
PACERS: Consistent, speed-oriented, rail-biased.
TROTTERS: High variance, break-prone, dislike tight quarters.
3.2 [GAIT-1] THE "BREAK RISK" PROTOCOL (Trotters Only)
Trigger: Analyzing any trotting race.
| Breaks in Last 5 | Risk Level | Action |
|------------------|------------|--------|
| 0 | LOW | Proceed normally |
| 1 | MODERATE | Note it, no penalty |
| 2 | HIGH | -5 points |
| 3+ | VERY HIGH | CAP at 2-Stars unless huge class edge |
3.3 [GAIT-2] THE "TROTTER POST POSITION" EXCEPTION
Standard Rule: Inside posts are best.
Trotter Exception: Many trotters DON'T like the rail on their left.
Action: In trots, give posts 3-5 a +2 bonus over post 1.
3.4 [GAIT-3] DRIVER GAIT SPECIALIZATION
If driver's trot win % is 5+ points LOWER than pace %: DEDUCT -3 points.
If driver's trot win % is 5+ points HIGHER than pace %: ADD +3 points.
3.5 [GAIT-4] THE "WIDE TROTTER" ADVANTAGE (Big Tracks Only)
Trigger: Trotting race at Mohawk or Meadowlands.
Logic: Trotters often break stride when crowded on the inside or behind breaking horses. Outside horses have clear air.
Action: If a Trotter is in Post 8-10 and has "Closing Speed" (passing horses in the stretch in recent lines):
UPGRADE +3 points (The "Stay out of Trouble" bonus).
IGNORE standard outside post penalty.
SECTION 4: TRIP HANDICAPPING PROTOCOLS
4.1 TRIP VALUE HIERARCHY
Small Tracks: Lead > Pocket > Second-Over > First-Over > Parked.
Big Tracks: Pocket > Second-Over > Lead > Closing (with sub-:27 kick).
4.2 [TRIP-1] PROJECTING THE RACE FLOW
Step 1: Identify the SPEED (Who leaves?).
Step 2: Predict Early Pace (Lone Speed vs. Contested).
Step 3: Place Each Contender (Where will they be at the half?).
4.3 [TRIP-2] THE "HIDDEN FORM" UPGRADE
Trigger: Horse showed good energy despite bad trip last out.
Parked out ("o") but finished within 3 lengths.
First-over into fast fractions.
Blocked/Boxed in with pace.
Action: ADD +5 to +8 points. The public dismisses these; you embrace them.
4.4 [TRIP-3] THE "POCKET TRIP" PREMIUM
On Half-Mile Tracks, if a horse figures to get the pocket (2nd, inside):
ADD +5 points. This is the "Garden Trip."
SECTION 5: SPEED & TIME PROTOCOLS
5.1 THE TRUTH ABOUT TIMES
Final Time: Misleading. Often dictated by the leader.
Quarter Times: The truth.
Last Quarter (L1/4): The most important metric at Big Tracks.
5.2 [SPEED-1] THE "SUCKED ALONG" TRAP
Trigger: Horse shows fast final time but was mid-pack/rail throughout.
Action: DEDUCT -5 points. They didn't earn that time; the race flow gave it to them.
5.3 [SPEED-2] THE "DID WORK" BONUS
Trigger: Horse raced on the lead, first-over, or made a huge sweep AND finished competitively.
Action: ADD +5 points. This horse has "grit."
SECTION 6: CLASS & VOLATILITY PROTOCOLS
6.1 [CLASS-1] THE "PURSE COMPARISON" METHOD
Compare today's purse to the horse's last 3 starts.
Drop 25%+: Significant class relief (+5 points).
Drop 10-25%: Moderate relief (+3 points).
Rise: Stepping up (-3 points).
6.2 [CLASS-2] THE "LIFETIME EARNINGS" CHECK
In conditioned races, check Lifetime Earnings.
If Horse A has $500k lifetime and Horse B has $50k, Horse A has "Back Class."
Action: Upgrade high-earners dropping in class.

SECTION 7: DRIVER & TRAINER PROTOCOLS
7.1 [DRIVER-1] THE "ELITE DRIVER" BONUS
Elite Drivers: Gingras, Miller, Dunn (Meadowlands); McNair, MacDonald, McClure (Mohawk); Bartlett, Stratton (Yonkers).
Action: If an elite driver picks up a horse they don't usually drive: ADD +5 points.
7.2 [DRIVER-2] THE "DRIVER CHOICE" TELL
Scenario: Top driver is listed on multiple horses and chooses the higher-odds horse.
Action: UPGRADE the chosen horse by +5 points.
7.3 [TRAINER-1] THE "NEW TRAINER" BOUNCE
Trigger: First start for a high-percentage barn (e.g., Burke, Auciello, Moreau) after a claim.
Action: ADD +5 points.
7.4 [TRAINER-2] THE "OWNER/OPERATOR" BONUS
Trigger: Trainer is listed as the Driver (e.g., Travis Cullen, Ed Hensley, Rick Zeron).
Logic: They know the horse better than a catch driver and know exactly when it is "live."
Action: ADD +4 points if the horse shows any sign of life in the last 2 starts (even if finishing 4th or 5th).
SECTION 8: FORM CYCLE PROTOCOLS
8.1 [FORM-1] THE "SECOND START BACK" PATTERN
Trigger: Horse returning from layoff (30+ days) makes their second start.
Action: If horse showed some interest in 1st start (closed late, flashed speed): ADD +5 points.
8.2 [FORM-2] THE "BEATEN FAVORITE" VALUE
Trigger: Horse was favorite last out, had a bad trip, and lost.
Action: ADD +3 points. The public will jump off; value increases.

HIGH: Amateur drivers, low-level trotters.
EXTREME: Bottom-level claimers (<$10k), multiple breakers in field. (Chaos)
