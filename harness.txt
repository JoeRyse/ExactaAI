# SECTION 3: GAIT-SPECIFIC PROTOCOLS

## 3.1 PACER VS. TROTTER FUNDAMENTALS

### PACERS (Lateral Gait)
- Move legs on SAME SIDE together (right front + right rear, then left front + left rear)
- Wear HOPPLES to maintain gait at speed
- MORE CONSISTENT than trotters
- LOWER break risk
- ~80% of North American races are paces

**Pacer Handicapping Priorities:**
1. Post Position (track-size dependent)
2. Projected Trip / Race Flow
3. Speed Figures / Final Times
4. Driver/Trainer Statistics
5. Class Level

### TROTTERS (Diagonal Gait)
- Move legs on OPPOSITE sides together (right front + left rear, then left front + right rear)
- Higher risk of BREAKING stride (going off-stride into gallop)
- Require MORE skill from drivers
- Post 1 can be PROBLEMATIC (trotters don't like rail on left)
- Higher variance = higher exotic payouts

**Trotter Handicapping Priorities:**
1. Break History (the "x" in past performances)
2. Post Position (NOTE: rail is NOT always best)
3. Driver's Trotter-Specific Win %
4. Projected Trip
5. Class / Speed

## 3.2 [GAIT-1] THE "BREAK RISK" PROTOCOL (Trotters Only)

**Trigger:** Analyzing any trotting race

**Check the Break Count ("x" markers in PPs):**
| Breaks in Last 5 | Risk Level | Action |
|------------------|------------|--------|
| 0 | LOW | Proceed normally |
| 1 | MODERATE | Note it, no penalty |
| 2 | HIGH | -5 points |
| 3+ | VERY HIGH | CAP at 3-Stars unless huge class edge |

**Break Context Matters:**
- Was break caused by interference? (Check trip notes)
- Did horse recover and still finish close?
- Is horse switching to a trotter-specialist driver?

## 3.3 [GAIT-2] THE "TROTTER POST POSITION" EXCEPTION

**Standard Rule:** Inside posts are best

**Trotter Exception:** Many trotters DON'T like the rail on their left
- Post 1 in trots can be problematic
- Posts 3-5 often optimal for trotters
- Check horse's past performances from various posts

**Action:** In trots, give posts 3-5 a +2 bonus over post 1

## 3.4 [GAIT-3] DRIVER GAIT SPECIALIZATION

**Reality:** Some drivers excel at one gait over the other

**Check for each driver:**
- Overall UDR (Universal Driver Rating)
- PACE-specific win %
- TROT-specific win %

**If driver's trot % is 5+ points LOWER than pace %:**
- DEDUCT -3 points when driving in trots

**If driver's trot % is 5+ points HIGHER than pace %:**
- ADD +3 points when driving in trots (trotter specialist)

# SECTION 4: TRIP HANDICAPPING PROTOCOLS

## 4.1 TRIP POSITIONS EXPLAINED

Harness racing is fundamentally about POSITION. Understanding the trip positions is critical.

### THE KEY POSITIONS
| Position | Description | Advantage/Disadvantage |
|----------|-------------|------------------------|
| **ON THE LEAD** | Front, on rail | Best tripâ€”shortest distance, controls pace |
| **POCKET** | 2nd, directly behind leader on rail | "Garden trip"â€”cover + shortest path |
| **FIRST-OVER** | Outside the leader, no cover | Difficultâ€”covering extra ground + no windbreak |
| **SECOND-OVER** | Outside, behind first-over horse | Excellentâ€”has cover, can tip 3-wide late |
| **THIRD ON RAIL** | 3rd position on inside | "Death hole" on small tracksâ€”boxed in |
| **PARKED OUT** | Racing outside entire mile | Energy-drainingâ€”marked with "o" in PPs |

### TRIP VALUE HIERARCHY (Small Tracks)
1. **On the Lead** â†’ BEST
2. **Pocket** â†’ Excellent
3. **Second-Over** â†’ Very Good (often overlooked value)
4. **First-Over** â†’ Difficult but can win with best horse
5. **Third on Rail** â†’ Poor
6. **Parked Out** â†’ Worst

### TRIP VALUE HIERARCHY (Big Tracks - Mile/7/8)
1. **Pocket** â†’ BEST (can time move perfectly)
2. **Second-Over** â†’ Excellent
3. **On the Lead** â†’ Good (but vulnerable to pressers)
4. **First-Over** â†’ Acceptable
5. **Closing from far back** â†’ Possible with fast last quarter

## 4.2 [TRIP-1] PROJECTING THE RACE FLOW

**Before making any selection, you MUST project how the race will unfold.**

**Step 1: Identify the SPEED**
- Which horses want the lead? (Check running style, early pace figures)
- Who has the best gate speed from inside posts?
- How many horses will contest the early lead?

**Step 2: Predict Early Pace**
| Scenario | Horses Wanting Lead | Expected Flow |
|----------|---------------------|---------------|
| LONE SPEED | 1 | Soft fractions, front-end wins |
| CONTESTED | 2 | Pressure, pocket or second-over wins |
| HOT PACE | 3+ | Fast fractions, closers benefit |

**Step 3: Place Each Contender**
- Where will your horse likely be at the quarter? Half?
- Can they get a good trip from their post?
- Will they be parked out? Boxed in?

## 4.3 [TRIP-2] THE "PARKED OUT" PENALTY

**Trigger:** Horse was "parked out" (marked with "o") in recent race

**Check:** How many times in last 5 races?
| Parked Count | Interpretation | Action |
|--------------|----------------|--------|
| 0-1 | Normal | No adjustment |
| 2-3 | Trip problems | Consider post improvement as + |
| 4-5 | Chronic issue OR very good horse | Dig deeperâ€”good sign if still competitive |

**Key Insight:** A horse that was parked AND still finished close is BETTER than their time suggests. This is HIDDEN FORM.

## 4.4 [TRIP-3] THE "HIDDEN FORM" UPGRADE

**Trigger:** Horse showed good energy despite bad trip last out
- Parked out but still finished within 3 lengths
- Went first-over and didn't fade
- Made middle move, got hung wide, still showed determination

**Action:** ADD +5 to +8 points. This horse is better than their last race looks.

**The Professional's Edge:** The public dismisses horses off bad trips. You should embrace them.

## 4.5 [TRIP-4] THE "POCKET TRIP" PREMIUM

**On Half-Mile Tracks:**
If a horse figures to get the pocket trip (2nd, inside, behind likely leader):
- ADD +5 points
- This is the safest, most advantageous trip

**Identifying Likely Pocket Horses:**
- Post 2 with tactical speed
- Post 3-4 if posts 1-2 lack gate speed
- Known "stalker" types

## 4.6 [TRIP-5] THE "SECOND-OVER" VALUE PLAY

**Statistical Reality:** Second-over trip is highly profitable, especially on small tracks.

**Why?**
- Has cover (saving energy)
- Can tip out 3-wide at the right moment
- Often overlooked by the public

**Identifying Likely Second-Over Horses:**
- Posts 4-6 on half-mile tracks
- Horses that typically don't leave aggressively
- "Come from behind" types from middle posts

**Action:** If you identify a legitimate second-over candidate with good form, ADD +3 points.

# SECTION 5: SPEED & TIME PROTOCOLS

## 5.1 UNDERSTANDING HARNESS TIMES

### FINAL TIME CONTEXT
Unlike thoroughbreds, harness races are almost always ONE MILE. Times are expressed as:
- 1:50.0 = 1 minute, 50 seconds (elite)
- 1:52.0 = Very fast
- 1:54.0 = Solid
- 1:56.0 = Moderate
- 1:58.0+ = Slower

**CRITICAL:** Final time alone is MISLEADING. A horse that was "sucked along" in a fast race gets an inflated time.

### QUARTER TIMES (The Truth)
Harness races are measured in quarters:
| Call | Distance | Typical Fast | Typical Moderate |
|------|----------|--------------|------------------|
| 1Q | Quarter | :27.0 | :28.0 |
| Half | Half Mile | :55.0 | :57.0 |
| 3Q | Three-Quarters | 1:23.0 | 1:25.0 |
| Final | Mile | 1:51.0 | 1:54.0 |

### LAST QUARTER TIME (Most Important)
The final quarter is the KEY handicapping metric at bigger tracks.

| Last Quarter | Interpretation |
|--------------|----------------|
| Sub :26.0 | Exceptional finishing kick |
| :26.0-:27.0 | Strong closer |
| :27.0-:28.0 | Moderate finish |
| :28.0+ | Weak finish OR doing all the work |

**At Meadowlands:** A horse that can close in sub-:27 is ALWAYS dangerous.

## 5.2 [SPEED-1] THE "SUCKED ALONG" TRAP

**Trigger:** Horse shows a fast final time but was mid-pack or worse throughout

**Reality:** They got carried to that time by faster horses in front. It's not their own speed.

**Action:** DEDUCT -5 points from horses whose best recent time came while:
- Racing 4th or worse at the half
- Never making a move
- Following fast fractions they didn't create

## 5.3 [SPEED-2] THE "DID WORK" BONUS

**Trigger:** Horse "did work" in their race:
- Raced on the lead OR
- Raced first-over (outside, no cover) OR
- Made a strong middle move

**AND** still finished competitively

**Action:** ADD +5 points. This horse EARNED their time.

## 5.4 [SPEED-3] TRACK-TO-TRACK TIME ADJUSTMENTS

Times at different tracks are NOT directly comparable due to track size and speed.

**Use Comparative Speed Ratings (CSR)** from programs to adjust.

**General Guidelines:**
| Track | Speed Context |
|-------|---------------|
| Meadowlands | Fastestâ€”elite times here mean something |
| Mohawk | Very fastâ€”comparable to Meadowlands |
| Yonkers | Moderateâ€”times not as fast |
| Pocono | Moderate-fast |
| Freehold | Moderate |
| Northfield | Varies with conditions |

**Action:** When horse is shipping from a slower track to a faster track, expect their time to improve slightly. Vice versa when shipping down.

## 5.5 [SPEED-4] THE "SPEED RATING" SCALE

Using TrackMaster or similar speed ratings:

| Rating | Quality Level |
|--------|---------------|
| 95+ | Elite / Open class |
| 90-94 | Strong / Preferred |
| 85-89 | Competitive |
| 80-84 | Mid-level |
| 75-79 | Lower claiming |
| <75 | Bottom tier |

**Key:** A 5-point speed rating edge is significant. A 10-point edge is commanding.

# SECTION 6: CLASS EVALUATION PROTOCOLS

## 6.1 HARNESS CLASS STRUCTURE

### RACE TYPES (Highest to Lowest)
1. **Stakes / Invitational** - Elite horses, highest purses
2. **Open Pace/Trot** - Top-level overnight races
3. **Preferred** - High-quality horses
4. **Conditioned** - Grouped by earnings (NW$X, NW$Y lifetime, etc.)
5. **Claiming** - Horses for sale at set price
6. **Maiden** - Non-winners

### CONDITIONED RACE NOTATION
- **NW$15000L5** = Non-winners of $15,000 in last 5 starts
- **NW$50000L** = Non-winners of $50,000 lifetime
- **NW3PM** = Non-winners of 3 pari-mutuel races
- **NW1PM** = Maidens / Non-winners

## 6.2 [CLASS-1] THE "PURSE COMPARISON" METHOD

**Best way to compare class:** Compare PURSE levels

**Action:**
1. Note the purse of today's race
2. Compare to purses in horse's recent starts
3. Dropping in purse = dropping in class (positive)
4. Rising in purse = rising in class (question mark)

| Purse Change | Interpretation | Adjustment |
|--------------|----------------|------------|
| Drop 25%+ | Significant class relief | +5 points |
| Drop 10-25% | Moderate relief | +3 points |
| Similar | Class neutral | Neutral |
| Rise 10-25% | Stepping up | -3 points |
| Rise 25%+ | Big step up | -5 points, need to prove |

## 6.3 [CLASS-2] THE "CLAIM DROP" ANALYSIS

**Trigger:** Horse dropping in claiming price

**In Harness Racing:** Unlike thoroughbreds, claiming drops are LESS suspicious
- Horses are often dropped to find their level
- Less indication of health issues than in thoroughbreds
- Still worth noting but not automatic red flag

**Action:** Note the drop, slight positive (+2 points) if horse was competitive at higher level

## 6.4 [CLASS-3] THE "LIFETIME EARNINGS" CHECK

Horses with high lifetime earnings racing in lower conditions often have "old class."

**Check:** Lifetime earnings vs. current class
| Lifetime Earnings | Current Class | Interpretation |
|-------------------|---------------|----------------|
| $500K+ | NW$15000L5 | MAJOR class edgeâ€”sleeping giant |
| $250K-500K | NW$10000L5 | Solid class edge |
| $100K-250K | NW$5000L5 | Moderate edge |

**The "Wake Up" Play:** High-earnings horse + Driver upgrade + Class drop = PRIME BET

# SECTION 7: DRIVER & TRAINER PROTOCOLS

## 7.1 DRIVER IMPORTANCE

In harness racing, the driver is MORE important than jockeys in thoroughbred racing.

**Why?**
- Driver controls pace/position throughout
- Driver decides when to make moves
- Driver-horse relationship matters
- Some drivers specialize by track or gait

### KEY DRIVER METRICS
| Metric | What It Measures | Elite Threshold |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| **UDR** | Universal Driver Rating (like batting avg) | .300+ |
| **Win %** | Percentage of wins | 15%+ |
| **ITM %** | In-the-money percentage | 50%+ |
| **Earnings** | Total purse money | Track-dependent |

## 7.2 [DRIVER-1] THE "ELITE DRIVER" BONUS

**Elite Drivers at Major Tracks:**
- **Meadowlands:** Yannick Gingras, David Miller, Dexter Dunn, Todd McCarthy
- **Mohawk:** Doug McNair, James MacDonald, Bob McClure, Louis Philippe Roy
- **Yonkers:** Jordan Stratton, Jason Bartlett, George Brennan
- **The Meadows:** Dave Palone, Aaron Merriman, Tony Hall

**Action:** If an elite driver picks up a drive on a horse they don't usually drive:
- ADD +5 points (they chose this horse for a reason)

## 7.3 [DRIVER-2] THE "DRIVER SWITCH" ANALYSIS

**Upgrade Trigger:** Horse gets a driver upgrade
| Previous Driver | New Driver | Action |
|-----------------|------------|--------|
| UDR .200-.250 | UDR .300+ | ADD +5 points |
| UDR .250-.280 | UDR .300+ | ADD +3 points |
| Catch driver | Trainer's primary driver | ADD +3 points |

**Downgrade Trigger:** Top driver jumping OFF this horse to drive another in same race
- DEDUCT -5 points. The driver knows something.

## 7.4 [DRIVER-3] THE "DRIVER CHOICE" TELL

**Powerful Angle:** When a top driver has multiple options and chooses the higher-odds horse

**Scenario:** Driver has rides in same race, picks Horse B (8/1 ML) over Horse A (3/1 ML)

**Action:** UPGRADE Horse B by +5 points. The driver's choice is telling.

## 7.5 [DRIVER-4] DRIVER/TRAINER COMBINATIONS

**Key Insight:** Some driver/trainer combos perform MUCH better together than their individual stats suggest.

**What to Check:**
- Combined win % when working together
- Recent success rate together (last 30 days)
- Do they regularly work together?

**Action:** If driver/trainer combo has 25%+ win rate together (vs. 15% individually):
- ADD +3 points

## 7.6 [TRAINER-1] TRAINER PATTERNS

**Key Trainer Stats to Track:**
| Situation | What to Check |
|-----------|---------------|
| Off Layoff | Trainer's win % first start after 30+ days |
| After Qualifier | Trainer's win % first race after Q |
| After Claim | Trainer's win % after claiming horse |
| Track-Specific | Trainer's win % at THIS track |

## 7.7 [TRAINER-2] THE "NEW TRAINER" BOUNCE

**Trigger:** Horse was recently claimed or transferred to a new trainer

**Check:** New trainer's record with claimed horses

**Action:**
- If new trainer has 20%+ win rate with new acquisitions: ADD +5 points
- First-start-for-new-barn is a known winning angle

# SECTION 8: FORM CYCLE PROTOCOLS

## 8.1 HARNESS FORM FUNDAMENTALS

**Key Reality:** Standardbreds are REMARKABLY CONSISTENT compared to thoroughbreds.
- They race frequently (often weekly)
- Good horses perform well week after week
- Look for PATTERNS, not single races

## 8.2 [FORM-1] THE "RECENT FORM" CHECK

**Examine Last 3-5 Races:**
| Pattern | Interpretation | Action |
|---------|----------------|--------|
| Consistent finishes (1st-3rd) | In form | Proceed confidently |
| Improving finishes (5th â†’ 3rd â†’ 2nd) | Peaking | PRIME BET (+5 points) |
| Declining finishes (1st â†’ 3rd â†’ 5th) | Form dipping | -5 points |
| Mixed (1st, 6th, 2nd, 8th) | Erraticâ€”check trips | Dig into trip notes |

## 8.3 [FORM-2] THE "BEATEN FAVORITE" VALUE

**Trigger:** Horse was favorite in last 2-3 races and lost

**Public Reaction:** They bet against the horse ("it can't win")

**Reality:** Often, the horse had bad trips or bad luck

**Your Reaction:**
1. Check WHY the horse lost (break? parked? interference?)
2. If explainable bad luck: ADD +3 points (value play)
3. By 3rd or 4th race back, you often get double-digit odds

## 8.4 [FORM-3] THE "LAYOFF" PROTOCOL

**Trigger:** Horse returning from layoff (30+ days)

### LAYOFF RISK SCALE
| Days Off | Risk Level | Adjustment |
|----------|------------|------------|
| 14-30 | Low | Neutral |
| 31-60 | Moderate | -3 points unless qualifier looked sharp |
| 61-90 | Elevated | -5 points |
| 90+ | High | -7 points, need to see |

### THE QUALIFIER EDGE
**Professional Insight:** Qualifiers are RACES. Most horses don't know the difference.

**If horse qualified before return:**
- Watch the qualifier time and trip
- A sharp qualifier (within 3 seconds of race speed) = ready to fire
- "He may need a race" crowd often creates VALUE

**Action:** If qualifier was sharp, IGNORE layoff penalty. Others will discount; you won't.

## 8.5 [FORM-4] THE "SECOND START BACK" PATTERN

**Reality:** Many horses improve significantly in their SECOND start after layoff.

**Why?**
- First race back often reveals fitness
- Trainer now knows where horse stands
- Horse gets race timing back

**Action:** If horse showed SOME effort first back but didn't win:
- ADD +5 points for second start back
- Especially if now facing same or easier class
